AC & Cooling
Why is my AC running but not cooling?

The most common causes are:

  • Dirty or clogged air filter (check and replace first — it's free)
  • Low refrigerant (a sign of a leak — requires a licensed tech)
  • Frozen evaporator coil (often caused by low airflow or refrigerant issues)
  • Failing compressor or capacitor
  • Dirty condenser coils (the outdoor unit)

Our $89 diagnostic will pinpoint the exact cause and we'll quote you before any work begins.

How often should I replace my air filter?

For most homes: every 1–3 months. Homes with pets, allergies, or heavy dust may need monthly changes. A dirty filter is the #1 cause of HVAC inefficiency and early system failure — it's the easiest and cheapest thing you can do to protect your system.

When should I repair vs. replace my AC?

A good rule of thumb: if the repair cost is more than 50% of the cost of a new unit, and the system is over 10 years old, replacement usually makes more financial sense. Modern systems are significantly more energy-efficient, so the savings on electricity often offset the replacement cost within a few years.

Our technicians will give you an honest assessment — we'll tell you what we'd do if it were our own home.

Heating
Why won't my heater turn on?

Common causes include:

  • Thermostat set incorrectly or batteries dead
  • Tripped circuit breaker
  • Pilot light out (older gas furnaces)
  • Clogged filter reducing airflow and triggering a safety shutoff
  • Ignitor failure or flame sensor issue

Check your thermostat and breaker panel first. If those look fine, give us a call — we'll diagnose it fast.

What is the average lifespan of a furnace or heat pump?

Gas furnaces: 15–20 years with proper maintenance. Heat pumps: 12–15 years. Air conditioners: 12–16 years. Regular tune-ups (once or twice a year) can add years to any system's life and dramatically reduce major repair frequency.

Maintenance Plans
What’s included in a tune-up?

A Lone Star HVAC tune-up includes:

  • Clean and inspect evaporator and condenser coils
  • Check and top off refrigerant levels
  • Inspect and tighten all electrical connections
  • Lubricate all moving parts
  • Test capacitors, contactors, and relays
  • Inspect heat exchanger (for gas systems)
  • Check thermostat calibration
  • Replace standard 1-inch air filter (filter included)
  • Full system performance report
How often should my HVAC system be serviced?

Twice a year is ideal: once in spring to prepare your AC for summer, and once in fall to prepare your heating system for winter. At minimum, once a year. Regular maintenance is the single biggest thing you can do to prevent emergency breakdowns and extend system life.
